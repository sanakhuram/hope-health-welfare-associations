About Us

Creating Opportunities, Shaping Lives

The Hope and Health Welfare Association, an autonomous non-governmental and non-profit entity, was established by the late Haji Muhammad Abdullah, a visionary philanthropist. His steadfast dedication to effectuating substantive and enduring advancements in the lives of the underprivileged endures as a testament to his legacy, continually inspiring the organization's mission to engender hope and foster a more auspicious future for those in need.

Our Foundation and Affiliations

Established in 2010, our association is a registered entity with the registration number 786/SDC/17. We are proud to be affiliated with prestigious organizations, ensuring the highest standards of education and training:

  • Skill Development Council Punjab
  • National Training Bureau
  • Ministry of Federal Education and Professional Training, Government of Pakistan

Our Facilities

The association comprises two primary units, each dedicated to different aspects of community support:

Abdullah General Hospital

Providing comprehensive medical care, Abdullah General Hospital is committed to ensuring healthcare accessibility for all. Here are some of the key services we offer:

  • Free Dialysis
    Our hospital offers free dialysis services to patients in need.
  • Critical Care Unit (CCU) Facility
    Our state-of-the-art Critical Care Unit (CCU) is equipped with advanced medical technology and staffed by highly trained professionals.
  • Outpatient Department (OPD) Services
    In addition to specialized treatments, Abdullah General Hospital provides free medicines and consultations in the outpatient department (OPD).

Islamic Education and Vocational Training Institute

This institute focuses on both religious and vocational education, fostering an environment where individuals can develop both spiritually and professionally. Courses offered include stitching and sewing, embroidery and embellishment, cooking and culinary arts, beautician training, and basic computer skills. Additionally, the institute places a strong emphasis on Islamic education, ensuring character building and spiritual growth.

Our Leadership

Mr. & Mrs. Muhammad Akram

Owners of a manufacturing industry, they bring extensive experience in business management and strategic planning to the association.

Dr. Muhammad Safdar

A consultant urologist (FRCS Surgery) based in the UK, he brings a wealth of medical expertise and leadership skills to the organization.

Mrs. Zahida Akram

“A Beacon of Women Empowerment”

Mrs. Zahida Akram is the driving force behind the Hope and Health Welfare Association, an organization dedicated to empowering underprivileged women and girls from Okara and nearby villages in Pakistan. Her unwavering commitment and tireless efforts have transformed the lives of countless women, providing them with the skills and confidence to become self-reliant and support their families.

A Personal Connection

Mrs. Zahida Akram's connection to the women she helps is deeply personal. She is not just a leader but a mentor and friend to many. Her hands-on approach, from teaching classes to providing individual support, has created a nurturing and empowering environment. She is often seen working alongside her students, sharing in their successes and guiding them through challenges.

Impact on the Community

Under Mrs. Zahida Akram's leadership, the Hope and Health Welfare Association has become a beacon of hope for women in Okara and surrounding villages. The institute not only equips women with practical skills but also instills a sense of pride and self-worth. Graduates of the program have gone on to start their own businesses, secure employment, and become active contributors to their communities.

Future Aspirations

Looking ahead, Mrs. Zahida Akram aims to expand the reach of the Hope and Health Welfare Association. She envisions a future where every woman in Pakistan has the opportunity to achieve financial independence and self-sufficiency. Her continued support to the community and commitment towards creating meaningful programs have already set the foundation for these future goals.

Mrs. Zahida Akram's story is one of resilience, dedication, and unwavering belief in the potential of every woman. She truly is a beacon of women empowerment, inspiring others to dream big and achieve greatness.

Together, they bring extensive expertise from their respective professions and are volunteering to spearhead the charity's strategy, manage teams, and oversee welfare activities and outcomes. All operational costs are borne by the organization itself, ensuring dedicated and efficient use of resources.
