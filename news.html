Meet Our Students

Meet Javaria Amir

Javaria Amir, a dedicated mother of two and a respected councilor in her area, is currently pursuing a computer course at our organization. With a strong aspiration to further her education, she plans to embark on an MBA program. Her commitment to personal and professional development serves as an inspiration to her community. Through her hard work and determination, Javaria is setting a powerful example for others, balancing her responsibilities while striving for academic and career excellence.

Meet Asma Chaudhry

Asma Chaudhry, hailing from Bhutto Colony in Okara, faced the daunting challenges of supporting her four sisters and two brothers after the passing of their father. Undeterred, she pursued vocational training in both computer skills and stitching at our organization. Her perseverance and dedication led her to secure a teaching position within the same institution. Asma now empowers other women with the skills she has mastered. She finds great support and cooperation among her colleagues, which has significantly contributed to her professional and personal growth.

Meet Saima Zafar

Saima Zafar, originally from Faisalabad, resides in Okara with her grandmother. After the passing of her father, she took on the responsibility of supporting her two younger brothers and sisters. Enrolled in our stitching course, Saima has successfully acquired the skills needed to support her family and secure a job. She is now a source of stability and pride for her household. Saima expresses immense happiness and satisfaction with her progress, finding fulfillment in her ability to provide for her loved ones while pursuing her professional ambitions.
